Design a **Place Detail Page** mockup for **Geometki** — a geospatial POI (Points of Interest) sharing platform. Use the site styles, color palette, typography, and component language established in our previous conversation.

---

## Page content blocks

The page contains the following blocks of information. Do not treat this as a layout spec — decide yourself how to best arrange, combine, or visually separate them for maximum clarity and aesthetic quality.

### 1. Hero
The opening section of the page. It establishes the place identity — title, visual impression, and primary actions.

### 2. Description
A long-form rich text block written by the author describing the place — its history, how to get there, what to expect. Can be several paragraphs long.

### 3. Info & Metadata
A structured block with key facts about the place:
- **Category** — with category icon (e.g. Monument, Café, Waterfall, etc.)
- **Author** — user avatar + name, clickable
- **Views** — total view count
- **Last edited** — date and time
- **Distance to me** — shown in km if geolocation is available (e.g. "9 724.4 km")
- **Coordinates** — displayed in DMS format (e.g. `52°57′57″N 36°4′16″E`), plain text
- **Open in maps** — three separate links: Yandex Maps, Google Maps, Wikimapia

### 4. Map
An embedded interactive map showing the exact location of the place with a marker. The user can interact with it.

### 5. Photo Gallery
A collection of photos uploaded by the author and other contributors. There may be 1 to 30+ photos.

### 6. Nearby Places
A block showing other geotags located close to this place. Uses the existing geotag card component (from our previous conversation) or a variation of it.

### 7. Activity Feed
A chronological log of actions related to this place — who added it, who edited it, who added photos, etc.

### 8. Comments
A threaded or flat comment section where users can leave text comments about the place.

---

## Requirements

- Draw inspiration from best-in-class place/location detail pages: Google Maps place pages, TripAdvisor, AllTrails trail pages, Atlas Obscura, Foursquare — use whatever patterns feel most appropriate.
- The page should feel rich and content-dense without being cluttered.
- Design for **both desktop and mobile** — please produce mockups for both breakpoints. The mobile version should be a fully designed screen, not just a description of how it adapts.
- Since this is a geo service, the **map should feel native to the page**, not like an afterthought widget.
- Make your own creative decisions on visual hierarchy, section ordering, typography scale, and spacing. The goal is a beautiful, modern, trustworthy place page.

Please produce a detailed high-fidelity mockup or wireframe of the full page.
